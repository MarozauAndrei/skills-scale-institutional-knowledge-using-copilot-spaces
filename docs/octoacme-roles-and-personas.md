# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers create user-centered solutions by researching user needs, designing interactions, and validating usability. They bridge user feedback and technical implementation to ensure products are intuitive and accessible.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Design user interfaces, flows, and interactions
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Collaborate on accessibility and inclusive design
- Iterate on designs based on user feedback and testing results
- Document design decisions and design systems

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and delightful user experiences
- Reduce user friction and support burden through good design
- Ensure alignment between user needs and product functionality

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with developers and product managers
- User research findings and testing reports
- Design specs and component documentation
- Collaboration on feature acceptance criteria

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Align on user needs and feature priorities
- **With Developers**: Review implementation against design specs and collaborate on interaction details
- **With Project Managers**: Input on timelines for design phases and user testing
- **With Quality/Testing**: Contribute to usability test scenarios and acceptance criteria

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business objectives and technical solutions by gathering requirements, analyzing processes, and documenting specifications. They ensure that business needs are clearly understood and properly translated into technical work.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Analyze processes and workflows to identify improvements
- Create detailed specifications and requirement documents
- Identify dependencies and potential risks in requirements
- Validate that solutions meet business objectives
- Support testing and acceptance against business criteria

### Goals
- Ensure business needs are clearly understood by technical teams
- Reduce rework and misalignment through clear requirements
- Bridge communication gaps between business and technical stakeholders
- Support data-driven decision-making

### Typical Communication
- Requirement specifications and business process documentation
- Stakeholder interviews and findings
- Traceability matrices linking requirements to implementation
- Acceptance testing and validation reports

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Refine product requirements and success metrics
- **With Project Managers**: Document dependencies and risks; support planning
- **With Developers**: Clarify requirements and resolve implementation questions
- **With Stakeholders**: Gather and validate business needs and acceptance criteria

---

## Release Managers

### Role Summary
Release Managers plan, coordinate, and execute releases to production. They ensure releases are well-coordinated, documented, and executed with minimal risk and maximum visibility across teams.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule release windows and deployment timelines
- Coordinate release activities across engineering, QA, and operations
- Prepare and maintain release notes and deployment documentation
- Manage pre-release checklist (code freeze, testing, sign-offs)
- Coordinate deployment execution and monitor post-deployment health
- Execute rollback procedures if issues arise
- Document lessons learned and process improvements

### Goals
- Execute reliable, repeatable releases with minimal production impact
- Maintain clear communication and transparency during releases
- Reduce time-to-market and cycle time through efficient release processes
- Build confidence in release quality and stability

### Typical Communication
- Release schedules and deployment windows
- Release notes and change logs
- Pre-release readiness reports
- Post-deployment verification and rollback documentation
- Release retrospectives and process improvements

### Key Interactions
- **With Project Managers**: Coordinate release timing with project milestones and deliverables
- **With Developers**: Ensure code is ready for release and coordinate final checks
- **With QA/Testing**: Verify testing completion and manage pre-release sign-off
- **With Operations/DevOps**: Execute deployment and manage infrastructure changes
- **With Product Managers**: Communicate release scope and timing to stakeholders

---

## Customer Success / Customer Advocates

### Role Summary
Customer Success professionals advocate for customer needs throughout the product lifecycle. They monitor adoption and satisfaction post-launch, collect feedback, and ensure customer success is a core consideration in product decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and communicate customer feedback and insights
- Monitor customer adoption, usage patterns, and satisfaction metrics
- Identify and escalate customer pain points and support issues
- Support customer onboarding and training
- Provide data on customer impact of new features
- Participate in product reviews and retrospectives
- Build and maintain strong customer relationships

### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction and retention
- Ensure customer voice is heard in product decisions
- Reduce support burden through proactive engagement
- Identify upsell and expansion opportunities

### Typical Communication
- Customer feedback summaries and sentiment analysis
- Adoption and usage metrics reports
- Support issue logs and escalation summaries
- Voice of customer insights in product reviews
- Customer success case studies and testimonials

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Share customer insights and feedback to inform prioritization
- **With Project Managers**: Escalate critical customer issues and risks
- **With Developers**: Communicate customer pain points and feature requests
- **With Support Teams**: Coordinate on customer issues and training needs
- **With Stakeholders**: Present customer success metrics and business impact

---

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- Recognize that teams may combine roles or have specialized variations based on project size and organizational structure.
- When roles are combined, ensure responsibilities are clearly divided and communication paths are maintained.
